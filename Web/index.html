Global Population, Emissions and Agricultural Growth

In its Fifth Assessment Report, the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change, a group of 1,300 independent scientific experts from countries all over the world under the auspices of the United Nations, concluded there's a more than 95 percent probability that human activities over the past 50 years have warmed our planet.‚Äù NASA.
Palatable visualizations are thought provoking and self-explanatory, instigating us to do our best to save our planet for the next generation. To investigate the above claim we used data wrangling in Python and visualization tools in Javascript (Leaflet, Plotly), investigating the biggest contributors to greenhouse gas emissions between the years 2000 - 2017. We chose the two most basic human activities; farming and population growth to collect data across countries. Official data was collected from FAO.com which was normalized and stored in SQL database for further processing. To uncover trend and co-relation between greenhouse gases (Methane, nitrous oxide & carbon dioxide) increased farming output and population growth. This project used JavaScript, HTML/CSS and Leaflet to create interactive visualizations and Github to host our data.
